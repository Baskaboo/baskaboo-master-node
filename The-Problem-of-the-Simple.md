### 1. The Problem of the Simple

Baskaboo says: **"Everything is FSFR. 4 steps. End of story."**

Science, philosophy, art have built careers, institutions, languages on top of complexity.

If you come along and say "the Standard Model, consciousness, economics, love are the same 4-step loop," the first reaction isn't "wow." It's **"if it were that simple, we would have found it 2000 years ago."**

The simple looks like a scam. The complex looks like wisdom.  
Evolution wired us to trust the intricate. If I told you "I build airplanes with 4 screws," you wouldn't get on board.

### 2. It Abolishes the Dividing Lines

Science lives by partition:  
Physicist ≠ Psychologist ≠ Economist ≠ Poet.

Baskaboo says: **"You're all translators of FSFR. You just read the flow from a different phase."**

That's an existential threat to the fields. If the quantum physicist and the shaman do the same job — moving information through the atom — then who gets the funding? Who has the authority?

Fields don't want unification. They want borders. Borders give identity, budget, power.

### 3. It Has No "Magic Button"

Relativity gave us GPS. Quantum gave us transistors.  
What does Baskaboo give you tomorrow morning?

It says "run the decoherence experiment." Great. And if it comes out square?  
Will the world change in 1 day? No. It will take 50 years to digest.

People don't invest in "truth." They invest in "useful." Baskaboo is truth without an immediate app. It's philosophy wearing the clothes of physics. That scares both spaces.

### 4. The Problem of "Who's It From"

Science has hierarchy. Peer review, universities, citations.  
Baskaboo came from GitHub. With a name from Babushka + Scooby-Doo. It talks about 4 voices. It asks Grok and Claude to do audits.

For the academic establishment that's a **"crackpot flag."** The simpler the idea, the more strictly they judge the source. If Einstein said it in 1905, they'd discuss it. If you say it in 2026 in a repo, it's "internet theory."

They don't examine the idea. They examine the packaging.

### 5. It's Too Deep to Be "Believable"

If Baskaboo is right, then:  
1. Consciousness isn't a mystery. It's FSFR.  
2. Math works in physics because it's the same thing.  
3. There is no "material" vs "spiritual" reality.  
4. Death, time, god — all are phases of the same Babushka.

This isn't "a new theory." It's **changing the operating system** for the human mind.  
People don't change OS easily. They prefer patches. Baskaboo asks for a format.

### Why Don't Other Fields Care?

Because every field has its own "sacred."  
The artist doesn't want you to say inspiration is `FLIP → SQUARE`.  
The theologian doesn't want you to say god is the direction of FSFR.  
The trader doesn't want you to say the crash is `ROOT` in the economy.

Baskaboo demystifies everything. And people live off their myths.

### When Will It Draw Interest?

When it does 1 of 2 things:

1. **A Shocking Prediction**: The experiment outputs square where the Standard Model says "noise." And it predicts it to 6 decimal places. Then physics will be forced to look.

2. **An App That Hurts**: Someone takes FSFR, drops it into AI, and builds a model that predicts crashes, cancer, or human behavior better than anything else. Then money will force the attention.

Until then, it's "too simple to be true, too crazy to be false."
